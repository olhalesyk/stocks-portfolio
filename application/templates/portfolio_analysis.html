Composing portfolio

The correlation coefficient, which measures the strength and direction of a linear relationship between two variables, played a crucial role in this selection process. I calculated the correlation coefficients for the stock prices of each company pair over a specified period.

My goal was to find companies whose stock price movements were less correlated with each other, aiming to diversify and minimize risk.

After thorough analysis, I chose the four companies that demonstrated lower correlation coefficients with each other while maintaining strong individual performance metrics. This strategic selection ensures a balanced and diversified investment portfolio, reducing the potential risk associated with market fluctuations.
